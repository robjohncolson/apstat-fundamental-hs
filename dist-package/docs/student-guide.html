üìö How It Works

This system teaches AP Statistics through proof of knowledge and peer consensus. Instead of having a teacher give you the "right" answer, you and your classmates discover it together!

The Process:

Question Selection: Pick from real AP Statistics curriculum questions
Answer Submission: Provide your answer with a confidence rating
Peer Attestation: Other students verify or challenge your answer
Consensus Formation: The system tracks agreement levels
Knowledge Emergence: The "correct" answer emerges through peer agreement

üîë Key Insight

There's no hidden answer key! Knowledge emerges through collective intelligence, just like in real scientific communities.

üéØ Understanding Question Types

Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ)

  • What you see: Standard AP-style multiple choice with options A-E
  • How to answer: Type the letter (A, B, C, D, or E)
  • Special feature: Your answer is encrypted until consensus forms
  • Why encrypted? Prevents bandwagon effects and encourages independent thinking

Free Response Questions (FRQ)

  • What you see: Open-ended statistical analysis problems
  • How to answer: Provide detailed explanations with your reasoning
  • Scoring: Peers rate your response on a 1-5 scale using the rubric
  • Consensus: Statistical analysis determines the "best" answer

‚öñÔ∏è Confidence Levels & Strategy

Your confidence level (1-5) affects your reputation score:

Level 1-2 (Low Confidence): "I'm mostly guessing"
Low risk, low reward - small reputation gain if right
Level 3 (Medium Confidence): "I think I know, but not sure"
Moderate risk/reward - standard reputation changes
Level 4-5 (High Confidence): "I'm very sure about this"
High risk, high reward - big reputation gain if right, big loss if wrong

‚ö†Ô∏è Strategy Tip

Be honest about your confidence! The system rewards accurate self-assessment. Overconfidence hurts your long-term reputation.

üèÜ Reputation System

Your reputation reflects your statistical knowledge and peer collaboration skills:

How You Gain Reputation:

  • Correct Attestations: Being right, especially when confident
  • Early Consensus: Being among the first to identify the correct answer
  • Minority Bonus: 1.5x bonus when you're right but in the minority initially
  • Peer Validation: Having your FRQ answers rated highly by classmates

How You Lose Reputation:

  • Wrong Answers: Especially when you were highly confident
  • Inconsistent Answers: Changing your mind frequently on similar questions
  • Poor FRQ Scores: Consistently low peer ratings on explanations

üéØ Goal

Build reputation through consistent, thoughtful participation. Quality over quantity!

üîç Learning the Invariants

The system operates under 13 mathematical rules called "invariants." Click the ? buttons throughout the interface to learn about them!

Key Invariants for Students:

  • Identity Integrity: Only registered users can participate
  • Progressive Quorum: Higher agreement = fewer votes needed
  • Confidence Weighting: Your confidence affects your score
  • Hash Validation: MCQ answers stay secret until consensus
  • Rate Limiting: Can't spam the same question repeatedly

üí° Educational Value

These invariants teach you about fairness, security, and mathematical rigor in decentralized systems!
